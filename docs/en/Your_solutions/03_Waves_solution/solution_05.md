# 🌊 5. Echo Ranging

---

## 📌 Problem

A sonar system sends out a sound pulse and receives the echo after **2.5 seconds**.  

If the speed of sound in water is **1500 m/s**, how far away is the object?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

In echo problems, the sound travels:

- from source → object  
- and back → source  

So the total time is for a **round trip**.

Distance formula:

$$
d = \frac{vt}{2}
$$

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Identify Given Values

- $v = 1500\ \text{m/s}$
- $t = 2.5\ \text{s}$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Apply Formula

$$
d = \frac{vt}{2}
$$

Substitute:

$$
d = \frac{1500 \cdot 2.5}{2}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 3 — Calculate

$$
1500 \cdot 2.5 = 3750
$$

$$
d = \frac{3750}{2}
$$

$$
d = 1875\ \text{m}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{d = 1875\ \text{m}}
$$

---

## 🎯 Visual (Echo Path)

<pre>
Source -----> Object
       <-----
   total time = 2.5 s
   one-way distance = d
</pre>

---

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- The measured time includes **going and returning**
- So we divide by 2 to find **one-way distance**

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Speed $v$ | 1500 m/s |
| Time $t$ | 2.5 s |
| Distance $d$ | 1875 m |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Echo time is always round-trip  
- Distance must be divided by 2  
- Sound travels very fast in water
