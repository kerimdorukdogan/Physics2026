# 🌊 4. Phase Difference

---

## 📌 Problem

What is the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of

$$
\frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

For a wave, the phase difference between two points separated by a distance $\Delta x$ is:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x
$$

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Use the Formula

We use:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\Delta x
$$

Given:

$$
\Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

Substitute:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\cdot \frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Simplify

The wavelength $\lambda$ cancels:

$$
\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}\ \text{rad}}
$$

---

## 🎯 Visual

<pre>
One wavelength:     |-------- λ --------|
Point separation:   |--- λ/3 ---|
Phase difference:        2π/3
</pre>

---

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

A full wavelength corresponds to a phase change of:

$$
2\pi \text{ rad}
$$

So if the distance is only:

$$
\frac{\lambda}{3}
$$

then the phase difference is only:

$$
\frac{1}{3}\cdot 2\pi = \frac{2\pi}{3}
$$

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Separation | $\lambda/3$ |
| Full-wave phase | $2\pi$ |
| Phase difference | $2\pi/3$ rad |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- One full wavelength corresponds to $2\pi$ radians  
- Phase is proportional to distance  
- A separation of $\lambda/3$ gives $2\pi/3$ radians
