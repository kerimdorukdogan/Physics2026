# 🌊 9. Beats

---

## 📌 Problem

Two sound waves have frequencies:

$$
f_1 = 256\ \text{Hz}, \quad f_2 = 260\ \text{Hz}
$$

👉 What is the **beat frequency**?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

When two waves with slightly different frequencies interfere, they produce **beats**.

The beat frequency is:

$$
f_{\text{beat}} = |f_1 - f_2|
$$

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Apply Formula

$$
f_{\text{beat}} = |f_1 - f_2|
$$

Substitute:

$$
f_{\text{beat}} = |256 - 260|
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Calculate

$$
f_{\text{beat}} = 4\ \text{Hz}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{f_{\text{beat}} = 4\ \text{Hz}}
$$

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- Two close frequencies interfere  
- The sound alternates between **loud and soft**  
- The rate of this variation is the **beat frequency**

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|----------|-------|
| $f_1$ | 256 Hz |
| $f_2$ | 260 Hz |
| Beat frequency | 4 Hz |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Beats occur from close frequencies  
- Beat frequency is the difference  
- We hear periodic loudness changes
