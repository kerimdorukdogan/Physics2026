# 🌊 8. Interference of Waves

---

## 📌 Problem

Two waves with the same amplitude and frequency interfere.

- What happens in **constructive interference**?  
- What happens in **destructive interference**?  

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

When two waves overlap, they combine using the **superposition principle**:

$$
y_{\text{total}} = y_1 + y_2
$$

Depending on their phase difference:

- Same phase → constructive interference  
- Opposite phase → destructive interference  

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Constructive Interference

When waves are **in phase**:

$$
\Delta \phi = 0
$$

The amplitudes add:

$$
A_{\text{total}} = A + A = 2A
$$

👉 Result: **maximum amplitude**

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Destructive Interference

When waves are **out of phase**:

$$
\Delta \phi = \pi
$$

The amplitudes cancel:

$$
A_{\text{total}} = A - A = 0
$$

👉 Result: **no wave (cancellation)**

---

## ✅ Final Results

- Constructive → amplitude increases (**2A**)  
- Destructive → amplitude becomes **0**

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- Waves do not destroy each other permanently  
- They temporarily combine and then continue  
- Interference depends on **phase difference**

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Type | Phase Difference | Result |
|------|------------------|--------|
| Constructive | 0 | Amplitude increases |
| Destructive | π | Cancellation |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Waves combine by superposition  
- Same phase → stronger wave  
- Opposite phase → cancellation
