# 🌊 7. Intensity Level (Decibel)

---

## 📌 Problem

A sound has an intensity of

$$
I = 10^{-6}\ \text{W/m}^2
$$

The reference intensity is

$$
I_0 = 10^{-12}\ \text{W/m}^2
$$

👉 What is the sound level in **decibels (dB)**?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

Sound intensity level is given by:

$$
\beta = 10 \log_{10}\left(\frac{I}{I_0}\right)
$$

where:

- $\beta$ = sound level in dB  
- $I$ = intensity  
- $I_0$ = reference intensity  

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Substitute Values

$$
\beta = 10 \log_{10}\left(\frac{10^{-6}}{10^{-12}}\right)
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Simplify the Fraction

$$
\frac{10^{-6}}{10^{-12}} = 10^{6}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 3 — Apply Logarithm

$$
\log_{10}(10^6) = 6
$$

So:

$$
\beta = 10 \cdot 6
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 4 — Final Calculation

$$
\beta = 60\ \text{dB}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\beta = 60\ \text{dB}}
$$

---

## 🎯 Visual (Intensity Scale)

<pre>
10^-12  →  0 dB   (threshold of hearing)
10^-6   →  60 dB  (normal conversation)
</pre>

---

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- Decibel scale is **logarithmic**  
- A large change in intensity becomes a smaller change in dB  
- $10^6$ times more intense → only 60 dB  

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Intensity $I$ | $10^{-6}$ W/m² |
| Reference $I_0$ | $10^{-12}$ W/m² |
| Ratio | $10^6$ |
| Sound level | 60 dB |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Decibel uses logarithmic scale  
- Ratio of intensities matters  
- Large ratios become manageable values
