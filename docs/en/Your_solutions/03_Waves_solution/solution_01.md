## 📌 Problem

A sound wave in air has a frequency of **440 Hz**.  

- What is its wavelength in air, where the speed of sound is **343 m/s**?  
- What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is **1482 m/s**?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

The basic wave relation is:

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

So:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Wavelength in Air

Given:

- $f = 440\ \text{Hz}$
- $v_{\text{air}} = 343\ \text{m/s}$

Use:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

Substitute:

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78\ \text{m}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Wavelength in Water

Given:

- $f = 440\ \text{Hz}$
- $v_{\text{water}} = 1482\ \text{m/s}$

Use:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

Substitute:

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37\ \text{m}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answers

### In air

$$
\boxed{\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.78\ \text{m}}
$$

### In water

$$
\boxed{\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37\ \text{m}}
$$

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- Frequency is determined by the source → **does not change**  
- Wave speed depends on the medium  

Since:

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

- higher speed → larger wavelength  

So the wavelength is much larger in water.

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Medium | Speed $v$ (m/s) | Frequency $f$ (Hz) | Wavelength $\lambda$ (m) |
|--------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| Air | 343 | 440 | 0.78 |
| Water | 1482 | 440 | 3.37 |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Wave speed, frequency, and wavelength are related  
- Frequency stays constant across media  
- Higher speed → larger wavelength
