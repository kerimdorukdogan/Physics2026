# 🌊 10. Sound Intensity vs Distance

---

## 📌 Problem

A sound source emits waves uniformly in all directions.  

At a distance of **2 m**, the intensity is:

$$
I_1 = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{W/m}^2
$$

👉 What is the intensity at a distance of **6 m**?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

Sound intensity follows the **inverse square law**:

$$
I \propto \frac{1}{r^2}
$$

So:

$$
\frac{I_2}{I_1} = \left(\frac{r_1}{r_2}\right)^2
$$

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Identify Values

- $I_1 = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{W/m}^2$
- $r_1 = 2\ \text{m}$
- $r_2 = 6\ \text{m}$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Apply Formula

$$
I_2 = I_1 \left(\frac{r_1}{r_2}\right)^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
I_2 = 1.0 \times 10^{-4} \left(\frac{2}{6}\right)^2
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 3 — Calculate

$$
\frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3}
$$

$$
\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{9}
$$

$$
I_2 = \frac{1.0 \times 10^{-4}}{9}
$$

$$
I_2 \approx 1.11 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{W/m}^2
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{I_2 \approx 1.1 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{W/m}^2}
$$

---

## 🎯 Visual (Inverse Square Law)

<pre>
Distance:   2 m        6 m
Intensity:  strong  →  much weaker
          (I)          (I/9)
</pre>

---

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- Sound spreads over a larger area as distance increases  
- Area grows as $r^2$  
- So intensity decreases as $\frac{1}{r^2}$  

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Distance | Intensity |
|----------|----------|
| 2 m | $1.0 \times 10^{-4}$ |
| 6 m | $1.1 \times 10^{-5}$ |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Intensity follows inverse square law  
- Increasing distance reduces intensity quickly  
- Tripling distance reduces intensity by 9 times
