# 🎸 2. String Harmonics

---

## 📌 Problem

A guitar string is **64 cm long** and has a fundamental frequency (one antinode) of **330 Hz**.  

👉 What is the **speed of the wave** on this string?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

For a stretched string:

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

For the **fundamental mode**:

- The string has **one antinode**
- The wavelength is:

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Convert Length

Given:

$$
L = 64\ \text{cm} = 0.64\ \text{m}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Find Wavelength

For the fundamental mode:

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

$$
\lambda = 2 \cdot 0.64 = 1.28\ \text{m}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 3 — Calculate Wave Speed

Use:

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

Substitute:

$$
v = 330 \cdot 1.28
$$

$$
v = 422.4\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{v \approx 422\ \text{m/s}}
$$

---
## 🎯 Visual (Fundamental Mode)

<pre>
Node           Antinode           Node
|--------------()--------------|
</pre>
---

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- The ends of the string are **fixed → nodes**
- The middle vibrates the most → **antinode**
- In the fundamental mode, the string fits **half a wavelength**

So:

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|----------|------|
| Length $L$ | 0.64 m |
| Wavelength $\lambda$ | 1.28 m |
| Frequency $f$ | 330 Hz |
| Speed $v$ | 422 m/s |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Fundamental mode → half wavelength on string  
- Wavelength is twice the string length  
- Wave speed depends on frequency and wavelength
