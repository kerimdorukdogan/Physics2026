# 🌊 6. Doppler Effect

---

## 📌 Problem

A car moves toward a stationary observer with a speed of **20 m/s**.  
The car emits a sound with a frequency of **500 Hz**.  

If the speed of sound in air is **340 m/s**, what frequency does the observer hear?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

For a moving source and stationary observer:

$$
f' = f \cdot \frac{v}{v - v_s}
$$

where:

- $f'$ = observed frequency  
- $f$ = source frequency  
- $v$ = speed of sound  
- $v_s$ = speed of the source  

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Identify Given Values

- $f = 500\ \text{Hz}$
- $v = 340\ \text{m/s}$
- $v_s = 20\ \text{m/s}$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Apply Formula

$$
f' = f \cdot \frac{v}{v - v_s}
$$

Substitute:

$$
f' = 500 \cdot \frac{340}{340 - 20}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 3 — Calculate

$$
340 - 20 = 320
$$

$$
f' = 500 \cdot \frac{340}{320}
$$

$$
f' = 500 \cdot 1.0625
$$

$$
f' = 531.25\ \text{Hz}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{f' \approx 531\ \text{Hz}}
$$

---

## 🎯 Visual (Approaching Source)

<pre>
Car ---> ---> ---> Observer
Waves compressed → higher frequency
</pre>

---

## 🔍 Physical Interpretation

- The source moves **toward** the observer  
- Waves get **compressed**  
- Wavelength decreases  
- Frequency increases  

---

## 📊 Quick Summary

| Quantity | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Source frequency | 500 Hz |
| Source speed | 20 m/s |
| Sound speed | 340 m/s |
| Observed frequency | 531 Hz |

---

## 📝 What we learned

- Motion changes observed frequency  
- Approaching → higher frequency  
- Receding → lower frequency
