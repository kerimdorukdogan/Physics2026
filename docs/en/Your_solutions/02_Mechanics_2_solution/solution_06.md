# 🎾 6. Energy Dissipation

---

## 📌 Problem

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of **2.0 m**.  
After each bounce, it loses **30% of its mechanical energy**.  

👉 To what height does it rise after the **second bounce**?

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

- Mechanical energy is proportional to height:
  
$$
E = mgh
$$

👉 So:

$$
E \propto h
$$

- If energy decreases, height decreases in the same ratio.

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — First Bounce

Energy after bounce:

$$
E_1 = 0.7 E_0
$$

So height becomes:

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot 2.0
$$

$$
h_1 = 1.4\ \text{m}
$$

---

## 🚀 Step 2 — Second Bounce

Again, lose 30%:

$$
E_2 = 0.7 E_1
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \cdot 1.4
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.98\ \text{m}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Answer

$$
\boxed{h \approx 0.98\ \text{m}}
$$

---

## 🔁 Alternative (Shortcut)

Each bounce multiplies height by **0.7**:

$$
h = 2.0 \cdot (0.7)^2
$$

$$
h = 2.0 \cdot 0.49 = 0.98\ \text{m}
$$
