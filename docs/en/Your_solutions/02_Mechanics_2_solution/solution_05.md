# 🚗 5. Inelastic Collision

---

## 📌 Problem

> A **70 kg runner** moving at **3 m/s** jumps onto a **140 kg stationary cart**.  
>
> What is the **final speed** of the system?  
> Is **kinetic energy conserved**? Explain.

---

<details>
<summary>📖 🔎 Click to see theory</summary>

### Inelastic Collision

- Momentum is **conserved** ✅  
- Kinetic energy is **NOT conserved** ❌  

</details>

---

## 🚀 Step 1 — Momentum Conservation

Use:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f
$$

---

### Given

- $m_1 = 70\ \text{kg}$  
- $v_1 = 3\ \text{m/s}$  
- $m_2 = 140\ \text{kg}$  
- $v_2 = 0$

---

### Substitute

$$
70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0 = (70 + 140)v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 v_f
$$

$$
v_f = 1\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## ✅ Final Speed

$$
\boxed{v_f = 1\ \text{m/s}}
$$

---

## ⚡ Step 2 — Energy Check

### Initial energy

$$
KE_i = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2
$$

$$
KE_i = 315\ \text{J}
$$

---

### Final energy

$$
KE_f = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2
$$

$$
KE_f = 105\ \text{J}
$$

---

## ❌ Is Energy Conserved?

No.

- Initial: **315 J**  
- Final: **105 J**

👉 Energy is **lost**

---

## 🧠 Physical Insight

<div style="background-color:#0f172a;padding:12px;border-left:5px solid #22c55e;border-radius:6px">

- Objects stick together → inelastic collision  
- Some energy converts to heat / deformation  
- Momentum stays constant  

</div>
